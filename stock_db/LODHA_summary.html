Regulatory Environment• The regulatory landscape of the Indian real estate sector is primarily governed by the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA), which came into full effect on May 1, 2017. This landmark legislation aims to enhance transparency, accountability, and consumer protection. A key provision of RERA is the mandatory registration of all residential and commercial projects with a land area exceeding 500 square meters or more than eight apartments. Furthermore, the Act mandates that developers deposit 70% of the funds collected from homebuyers into a separate escrow account to be used exclusively for the construction of that specific project, thereby preventing the diversion of funds. This has brought significant financial discipline to the sector.
Regulatory Environment• RERA has established a robust framework for compliance and dispute resolution, significantly impacting developers' operations. Non-compliance with the Act attracts hefty penalties; for instance, failure to register a project can lead to a penalty of up to 10% of the project's estimated cost, and continued violation could lead to imprisonment. Real estate agents are also required to register, with penalties for non-compliance starting from ₹10,000 per day, up to 5% of the property's cost. The Act also mandates a defect liability period of five years for developers, enhancing construction quality. Furthermore, any alterations to the sanctioned plans require the consent of at least two-thirds of the allottees. These regulations have fostered a more transparent and buyer-centric market.
