Regulatory EnvironmentThe regulatory framework for housing finance companies (HFCs) in India is primarily overseen by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), which took over the regulatory powers from the National Housing Bank (NHB) following the Finance Act of 2019. The RBI is in the process of harmonizing the regulations for HFCs with those applicable to Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) in a phased manner. A key requirement introduced by the RBI is the minimum Net Owned Fund (NOF) for HFCs, which is set at ₹20 crore. Existing HFCs with a lower NOF were required to achieve ₹15 crore by March 31, 2022, and the full ₹20 crore by March 31, 2023, to continue their operations and retain their certificate of registration.
Regulatory EnvironmentTo ensure financial stability, the RBI has mandated that HFCs maintain a liquidity buffer in the form of a Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) to withstand a 30-day stress scenario. Furthermore, HFCs are required to have at least 60% of their total assets in housing finance by March 31, 2024. The framework also places restrictions on lending to real estate developers, with exposure to group companies in the real estate business capped at 15% of owned funds for a single entity and 25% for all such entities within a group. The Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio is capped at 90% for smaller loans and 80% for loans exceeding ₹30 lakh, aiming to minimize credit risk in the sector.
