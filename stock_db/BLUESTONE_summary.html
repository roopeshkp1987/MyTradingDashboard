Regulatory EnvironmentThe Indian jewellery sector operates under a comprehensive regulatory framework aimed at ensuring consumer protection, quality standardization, and fair trade. A cornerstone of this is the mandatory hallmarking of gold and silver jewellery, which came into full effect in June 2021 under the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) Act, 1986, and the Hallmarking Act, 2019. T hese regulations mandate that jewellers must be registered with the BIS and sell precious metal articles only after they are certified for purity at BIS-certified assaying and hallmarking centers. For instance, 14K gold must contain 58.3% pure gold. T he hallmark itself contains four distinct marks: the BIS logo, the purity grade, the assayer's mark, and the jeweller's identification mark.

To make Indian jewellery more competitive globally, export incentives such as a 4% interest subsidy on exports are in place.
