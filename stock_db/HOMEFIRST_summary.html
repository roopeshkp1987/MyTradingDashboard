Regulatory EnvironmentSince the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) assumed regulation of Housing Finance Companies (HFCs) from the National Housing Bank in August 2019, the sector has been subject to a revised and more harmonized framework. A key change implemented in October 2020 was the doubling of the minimum Net Owned Fund (NOF) requirement to ₹20 crore, to be met by March 31, 2023. The framework also mandates that by March 31, 2024, at least 60% of an HFC's total assets must be in housing finance. Furthermore, the RBI introduced Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio caps, limiting loans to 90% of the property value for amounts up to ₹30 lakh and 80% for loans exceeding this threshold. More recent amendments, effective from January 1, 2025, further align HFC regulations with those for Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs), permitting HFCs to issue co-branded credit cards and participate in currency derivatives.
