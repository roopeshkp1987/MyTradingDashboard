OverviewAegis Vopak Terminals Ltd (AVTL), a joint venture between Aegis Logistics (51%) and Royal Vopak of the Netherlands (49%), is India's largest independent third-party owner and operator of tank storage terminals for liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) and liquid products. A s of December 2024, the company managed a network of 20 terminals across six major Indian ports, including Haldia, Kandla, Pipavav, Mangalore, and Kochi. T his infrastructure provides a total liquid storage capacity of approximately 1.68 million cubic meters and an LPG static capacity of 70,800 metric tons. F or the fiscal year ending March 31, 2026, AVTL reported a 52.1% increase in consolidated net profit to ₹341.9 crores, with revenue from operations growing 17% to ₹923.1 crores. The c ompany's workforce included 392 full-time employees as of June 30, 2024.
Business Segments & Products/ServicesAegis Vopak Terminals Ltd operates through two primary business segments: the Gas Terminal Division and the Liquid Terminal Division. The Gas Division focuses on the storage and handling of LPG, including propane and butane, and accounted for approximately 44.5% of total revenue, or ₹276.35 crore, in fiscal year 2025. The Liqui d Terminal Division, which handles over 30 types of chemicals and more than 10 categories of edible and non-edible oils, petroleum products, and lubricants, is the larger segment, contributing around 55.5% of revenue, or ₹344.73 crore, in the same period. For the nin e months of fiscal year 2026, the liquid segment generated ₹319.40 crores (58.16% of revenue), while the gas segment contributed ₹229.73 crores (41.84% of revenue). The company ser ves over 400 clients across various sectors, including national oil marketing companies.
Market Share & Customer BaseAs India's largest third-party tank storage operator, Aegis Vopak Terminals Ltd holds a significant market position. As of December 31, 2024, the company commanded approximately 11.52% of India's total national LPG static capacity and about 25.53% of the third-party liquid storage capacity. Its strategically located terminals at five key ports handle a substantial portion of the nation's imports, specifically around 61% of LPG and 23% of liquid imports. The company has a diversified customer base of over 400 clients, which includes major national oil marketing companies (OMCs), traders, manufacturers, and fuel marketers from both public and private sectors. This broad base e nsures a high repeat business rate, averaging over 92%. Key competitors i n the broader logistics and storage sector include Container Corporation Of India Ltd, Delhivery Ltd, and Blue Dart Express Ltd.
Capacity DetailsAegis Vopak Terminals Ltd is undergoing significant capacity expansion under its "Project GATI" initiative. The company plans t o increase its LPG static capacity from 70,800 MT to 200,800 MT by FY26. A major project at Jawaharlal Nehru Port Authority (JNPA) involves a capital expenditure of ₹1,675 crores to add 318,100 cubic meters of liquid storage, a 77,286 MT LPG terminal, and a 35,000 MT per annum bottling plant, with the first phase expected to be operational in Q1 FY27. At Pipavav, the compa ny commissioned a 48,000 MT cryogenic LPG terminal in June 2025 and is developing India's first independent ammonia terminal with a 36,000 MT capacity, backed by a 15-year agreement with Hindustan Zinc. A new 82,000 MT cryog enic LPG terminal was also commissioned in Mangalore in June 2025. The long-term vision includes a capex plan of USD 5 billion by 2030.
