Regulatory EnvironmentIndia's aviation sector operates under a comprehensive regulatory framework managed by several key government bodies. The Ministry of Civil Aviation (MoCA) formulates national policies, while the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) is the primary entity for enforcing safety and operational compliance, including licensing and airworthiness standards. Econ omic aspects, such as airport tariffs, are regulated by the Airports Economic Regulatory Authority (AERA), established under the AERA Act of 2008. The government's Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) policy permits 100% FDI for non-scheduled services and up to 49% FDI via the automatic route for scheduled domestic airlines, with investments by foreign carriers also capped at 49%. Anti -competitive behavior is monitored under the provisions of the Competition Act, 2002.
