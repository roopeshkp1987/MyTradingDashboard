Regulatory EnvironmentThe regulatory environment for the Indian telecom sector has undergone a landmark transformation with the implementation of the Telecommunications Act, 2023, which officially came into force in June 2024. This new legislation replaces a framework that was nearly 140 years old, primarily governed by the Indian Telegraph Act of 1885. A central feature of this reform is the shift from a complex, multi-layered licensing system to a more streamlined, unified "authorisation" model, intended to simplify regulatory processes and improve the ease of doing business. The sector's oversight is managed by key bodies including the Department of Telecommunications (DoT) and the Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI), which was established on February 20, 1997. An amendment in the year 2000 also created the Telecom Disputes Settlement and Appellate Tribunal (TDSAT) to handle adjudicatory functions.
