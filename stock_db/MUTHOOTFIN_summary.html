Recent TrendsIn the second quarter of 2025, the Indian gold loan sector faced significant turbulence following the Reserve Bank of India's (RBI) introduction of draft guidelines on April 9, 2025, aimed at standardizing lending practices. Regulatory EnvironmentThe regulatory landscape for Muthoot Finance is primarily governed by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), which in April 2025, proposed draft guidelines for gold-backed loans to enhance regulatory clarity and streamline compliance across all lenders. These guideli nes, which followed the RBI's observations of irregularities in gold loan practices in September 2024, aim to unify fragmented older rules. Key proposals include linking loan amounts to borrowers' repayment ability, clearer guidelines for calculating the loan-to-value (LTV) ratio, and ensuring the 75% LTV limit applies throughout the loan term. While these r ules could increase operational complexity and slow down loan approvals, especially in rural areas, Fitch Ratings noted that large specialists like Muthoot Finance are well-positioned to adapt.
