Regulatory EnvironmentThe regulatory landscape of the Indian real estate sector is primarily governed by the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA). This landmark legislation was enacted to enhance transparency, ensure accountability, and protect the interests of homebuyers. Under RERA, it is mandatory for developers to register all projects with a specified minimum area with the respective state's Real Estate Regulatory Authority. This registration process requires the disclosure of comprehensive project details, including layouts, approvals, timelines, and financial information. The Act also mandates that developers deposit a significant portion of the project funds into a separate escrow account to prevent the diversion of funds and ensure timely project completion.
Regulatory EnvironmentRERA has introduced several key provisions that directly impact developers like Godrej Properties and empower homebuyers. For instance, developers are prohibited from charging more than 10% of the project's cost as an advance or application fee. A crucial requirement is that 70% of the funds collected from homebuyers must be maintained in a separate escrow account dedicated to the construction of that specific project. Furthermore, developers are mandated to provide all project-related information on the RERA website and keep it updated, enhancing transparency. The Act also defines the "carpet area," ensuring that buyers pay for the actual usable area. These regulations aim to standardize practices across the industry and build greater trust between developers and consumers.
