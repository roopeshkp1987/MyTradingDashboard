I n 2024, India's CDMO sector accounted for 8.7% of the global market. Sectors/IndustriesThe structure of India's CDMO market is heavily weighted towards Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient (API) and intermediate manufacturing, which is the largest revenue-generating segment. In 2024, API production held a dominant revenue share of 81.7%. The broader Indian pharmaceutical industry, valued at approximately USD 50 billion in FY 2023-24, provides a strong foundation for the CDMO sector and is projected to reach USD 130 billion by 2030. Key players in the competitive landscape include established firms like Syngene International, Piramal Pharma Solutions, Dr. Reddy's Laboratories, and Divi's Laboratories, which leverage the country's cost-effective manufacturing and skilled workforce.

For example, Sai Life Sciences is investing between ₹1,100 crore and ₹1,300 crore in capacity expansion by FY27 and maintains a revenue growth guidance of 15-20% CAGR. This tren d is part of a broader movement where India is evolving from a tactical supplier to a strategic partner for global pharma.
