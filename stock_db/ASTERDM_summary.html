Regulatory EnvironmentHospitals and healthcare providers in India must comply with a multifaceted array of specific regulations and standards governing operations, quality, and patient safety. While not mandatory, accreditation from the National Accreditation Board for Hospitals & Healthcare Providers (NABH) is becoming a benchmark for quality, assessing institutions on over 600 detailed parameters. Providers must also adhere to strict environmental laws, including the Biomedical Waste Management Rules which mandate the segregation of waste into four color-coded categories. Furthermore, the Consumer Protection Act empowers patients to seek legal recourse for medical negligence, adding a layer of accountability for providers.
