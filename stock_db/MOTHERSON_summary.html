Regulatory EnvironmentVehicle safety regulations have been significantly enhanced with the introduction of the Bharat New Car Assessment Program (Bharat NCAP), effective from October 1, 2023. This voluntary program assigns star ratings (1 to 5) based on performance in crash tests, including a frontal offset test at 64 km/h and a side impact test at 50 km/h. A 5-star rating requires a minimum of 27 out of 32 points for adult protection and 41 out of 49 for child protection. The upcoming Bharat NCAP 2.0, expected by 2027, will be even more stringent, expanding the assessment to five pillars and raising the 5-star threshold to 70 out of 100 points initially, and then to 80 points from 2029, driving demand for advanced safety components.
