Regulatory EnvironmentIndia has undertaken a significant overhaul of its upstream regulatory framework to attract investment and promote ease of doing business. The corne rstone of this reform is the Oilfields (Regulation and Development) Amendment Act, 2025, which came into effect on April 15, 2025. This amen dment replaces the previous dual-license system, which required separate approvals for exploration and production, with a single "petroleum lease" covering all activities. Building on this, the Ministry of Petroleum & Natural Gas notified the new Petroleum and Natural Gas Rules, 2025 (PNGR 2025) on December 9, 2025, further streamlining processes. A key fea ture of the new rules is the provision for long-term leases of up to 30 years, which can be extended for the entire economic life of a field, providing greater certainty for investors.
Regulatory EnvironmentThe new regulatory environment emphasizes faster approvals and a shift from criminal penalties to financial ones to encourage compliance and investment. Under the P etroleum and Natural Gas Rules of 2025, applications for petroleum leases are mandated to be decided within a 180-day timeframe. Violations now attract fines of up to ₹25 lakh, with an additional penalty of ₹10 lakh for each day of continued non-compliance, replacing the possibility of criminal liability. This aligns wit h a broader policy shift that began with the New Exploration Licensing Policy (NELP) in 1997, which offered incentives like a seven-year tax holiday to attract private investment. More recently, the Open Acreage Licensing Policy (OALP) of 2017 allows companies to nominate blocks for exploration, creating a more continuous opportunity window.
