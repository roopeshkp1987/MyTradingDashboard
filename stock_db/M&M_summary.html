Regulatory RiskMahindra & Mahindra faces significant regulatory challenges related to evolving emission standards, which could increase compliance costs and impact product development timelines. The company has a high concentration of diesel vehicles, accounting for over 70% of its SUV sales, making it particularly vulnerable to stricter regulations targeting diesel emissions. The proposed implementation of the Worldwide Harmonised Light Vehicles Test Procedure (WLTP) in India, potentially effective from April 2027, introduces more stringent testing conditions than the current Modified Indian Driving Cycle (MIDC). Additionally, discussions around Corporate Average Fuel Efficiency (CAFE) phase 3 and 4 norms present further hurdles; one proposal suggests a 33% reduction in average fleet emissions compared to CAFE-2 levels, which M&M has argued is an "enormous burden." The company has actively advocated for a deferral of the WLTP implementation and a more achievable CAFE-3 reduction target of 21%.
Market RiskM&M is exposed to intense competition and market volatility, which could pressure its pricing power and market share. In the passenger vehicle (PV) segment, the company, with a market share of 12.34% in FY25, faces formidable rivals such as Maruti Suzuki, the market leader holding a 40.25% share, and Hyundai with a 13.46% share. Despite M&M's strong PV sales growth of 19.95% in FY25, its competitors are also aggressive, with Tata Motors holding a 12.90% market share. In the crucial tractor segment, where M&M holds a dominant ~41% market share, the business is inherently cyclical and dependent on rural demand and monsoon patterns. The company has recently implemented price hikes for its tractors, effective from April 8, 2026, and for its Swaraj brand from April 21, 2026, citing rising commodity and input costs, which could impact demand.
Business RiskOperational continuity is threatened by significant supply chain vulnerabilities, particularly labor shortages at key vendors and reliance on specific commodities. The company could face up to a 15 percent reduction in its SUV production in a single month due to labor shortages at its suppliers, with one major vendor reportedly facing a 20-25 percent supply shortfall. This directly impacts the output from its manufacturing facilities, which have an installed capacity of around 57,000 petrol and diesel vehicles per month. In response, M&M has undertaken a comprehensive supply chain review, analyzing Rs 100,600 crore in purchases across 100,000 parts, identifying 82 "part-families" and nine commodities as high-risk due to geopolitical, logistical, and regulatory factors. This highlights the complexity and potential for disruption within its extensive procurement network.
