Regulatory EnvironmentThe primary regulatory body for the Indian pharmaceutical sector is the Central Drugs Standard Control Organization (CDSCO), which was established in 1940. A significant amendment in 2005 allowed clinical trials in India to be conducted concurrently with the same trial phase elsewhere in the world. The Patents (Amendment) Act of 2005 has also shifted the strategy of leading Indian companies towards developing new drugs and exporting to regulated markets. To encourage research and development, the government allows a weighted tax deduction of 200% for R&D expenditure. In May 2024, the CDSCO issued draft guidance on Good Distribution Practices (GDP) for pharmaceutical products.
