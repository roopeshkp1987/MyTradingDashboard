OverviewArchean Chemical Industries Limited, incorporated in 2009, is a prominent specialty marine chemical manufacturer in India with its headquarters in Chennai. The company operates an integrated production facility in Hajipir, Gujarat, leveraging the brine resources from the Great Rann of Kutch. As of March 31, 2024, the company had 288 employees. The nearby Jakhau Jetty, with a designed capacity of 5 million MT per annum, and Mundra Port facilitate its significant export operations. For the fiscal year 2024, the company reported a revenue decline of 7% and an operating profitability of 36.2%. The company's focus on a sustainable, solar evaporation production process for its key products has been a crucial factor in its operational efficiency and in securing long-term partnerships.
Business Segments & Products/ServicesArchean Chemical Industries' core business is the manufacturing and sale of specialty marine chemicals, categorized into a single 'Marine Chemicals' industry segment. The primary products are bromine, industrial salt, and sulphate of potash (SOP). For the fiscal year 2024, industrial salt contributed ₹728.12 crore to the standalone revenue, while bromine accounted for ₹605.28 crore. These products have diverse applications; bromine is utilized in pharmaceuticals, agrochemicals, and flame retardants, while industrial salt is a key raw material for the chlor-alkali industry. The company is also expanding into bromine derivatives through its subsidiary, Acume Chemicals Pvt Ltd, with a new facility having a capacity of 28,000 MTPA, of which phase 1 was commissioned on March 14, 2024.
Business Segments & Products/ServicesThe company's products cater to a wide array of industries including agriculture, pharmaceuticals, water treatment, and textiles. The sulphate of potash (SOP) it produces is a high-quality, low-chlorine fertilizer. In Q4 FY26, bromine volumes recovered to 3,731 metric tonnes, a 53% increase from Q2 FY26. For the full year, industrial salt volumes faced headwinds, with customer deferrals of approximately 120,000 tonnes and a loss of 250,000 tonnes due to logistical disruptions. The company has also diversified its interests by investing in the semiconductor space through its subsidiary, SiCSem Private Limited, which is setting up a Silicon Carbide based Compound Semiconductor fab with a proposed investment of ₹2067 crore.

This expansion is aimed at reducing imports into India and increasing exports, thereby generating foreign exchange for the country.
