Regulatory EnvironmentThe regulatory framework for depositories in India is primarily governed by the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) through the SEBI (Depositories and Participants) Regulations, 2018. Regulatory EnvironmentThe SEBI (Depositories and Participants) Regulations of 2018 introduced significant changes to the shareholding and governance structure of depositories. Under the new regulations, the maximum shareholding in a depository for any single entity, along with persons acting in concert, is capped at 5% of its paid-up equity share capital. However, an exc eption allows certain institutional investors, such as Indian and foreign stock exchanges, depositories, and banking companies, to hold up to 15%. An applicant pr oposing to establish a depository is now locked in for five years from the date of registration and can only hold up to 15% of the share capital, a change from the previous requirement where a sponsor had to hold at least 51%. Furthermore, de positories must maintain a minimum net worth of ₹100 crore, a figure significantly exceeded by CDSL, which reported a net worth of ₹1250.91 crore as of March 31, 2025.
