Regulatory EnvironmentIndia's regulatory framework for surveillance technology has been significantly strengthened to enhance national security and cybersecurity, directly impacting manufacturers and importers. Effective April 1, 2026, all internet-connected CCTV cameras sold in India must comply with stringent certification standards from the Standardisation Testing and Quality Certification (STQC) directorate and the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS). These rules were introduced by the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) in April 2024, providing a two-year transition period for the industry to adapt. The regulations mandate testing for vulnerabilities, secure firmware, and disclosure of the country of origin for key components, a move that has already led to over 500 camera models being certified and is expected to limit market access for non-compliant foreign brands.
Regulatory EnvironmentThe legal environment governing data privacy has become more stringent with the introduction of new legislation that organizations utilizing CCTV surveillance must adhere to. The Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, enacted on August 11, 2023, establishes a comprehensive framework for the collection, processing, and storage of personal data captured by surveillance systems. This act imposes significant responsibilities on entities, with potential penalties for non-compliance reaching up to ₹250 crore for failing to implement reasonable security safeguards to prevent data breaches. Prior to the DPDP Act, data protection was primarily governed by the Information Technology Act of 2000 and the SPDI Rules of 2011. These regulations mandate that organizations must inform individuals and obtain consent for data collection and usage.
