Industry or Sector Analysis

SubsectionDescription
Sectors/IndustriesThe Indian steel industry stands as a formidable force in the global market, holding the position of the world's second-largest crude steel producer with an output of 151.14 million tonnes (MT) in FY25. The sector is a significant contributor to the national economy, accounting for approximately 2% of the country's GDP and providing direct employment to over 600,000 people. Driven by robust domestic demand from infrastructure and construction, India's steel consumption is projected to grow by 8-9% in 2025. In line with this growth, the National Steel Policy has set an ambitious target to augment the nation's steel manufacturing capacity to 300 MT per annum and increase the per capita steel demand to 160 kg by the 2030-31 fiscal year, a substantial increase from the 97.7 kg recorded in 2023-24.
Recent TrendsA significant trend shaping the steel industry is the global push for decarbonization and the rise of "green steel," prompted by the sector's substantial environmental footprint, accounting for around 12% of India's total GHG emissions. This transition is accelerated by international regulations like the European Union's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM), which imposes a carbon tax on imports and could compress Indian steel exporters' profits by an estimated US$60 to US$165 per metric tonne between 2026 and 2034. Consequently, there is a growing emphasis on shifting from traditional coal-based blast furnaces, which account for about 43% of India's steel output, towards cleaner technologies like electric arc furnaces (EAFs). India has set a long-term goal to achieve net-zero emissions by 2070 and is taking steps to define standards for green steel, which the country officially adopted in December 2024.
Regulatory EnvironmentThe Indian government actively fosters growth in the steel sector through a structured regulatory framework, headlined by the National Steel Policy (NSP) of 2017. This policy aims to create a globally competitive industry, setting targets to achieve 300 MT of production capacity and 160 kg per capita steel consumption by 2030-31. A key initiative to bolster domestic manufacturing is the Production Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme for Specialty Steel, approved in July 2021 with a financial outlay of ₹6,322 crore. T his scheme, which offers incentives ranging from 4% to 15% on incremental sales, has attracted committed investments of ₹43,874 crores and is expected to add 14.3 million tonnes of specialty steel capacity. To ensure product standards, the government has implemented a Quality Control Order (QCO), which has so far notified 151 Indian Standards for various steel products.
