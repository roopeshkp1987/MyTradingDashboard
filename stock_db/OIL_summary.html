Regulatory EnvironmentThe regulatory framework for India's oil and gas sector is primarily governed by the Oilfields (Regulation and Development) Act, 1948, and the Petroleum and Natural Gas Rules, 1959 (PNGR). However, the government has recently introduced significant updates, including the Oilfields (Regulation and Development) Amendment Act, 2025, and the Petroleum and Natural Gas Rules, 2025. These new regulations aim to streamline the licensing and leasing regime. Key approvals required for oil and gas projects include a Petroleum Exploration License (PEL) and a Petroleum Mining Lease (PML). The sector is also subject to a range of other regulations, including the Environmental Protection Act, 1986, and obtaining an Importer Exporter Code (IEC) is mandatory for companies involved in crude oil imports.
Regulatory EnvironmentThe Indian government allows 100% Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) in the exploration and production of oil and natural gas under the automatic route. This policy is designed to attract foreign capital and technology to the sector. For midstream and downstream operations, the Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board (PNGRB) is the primary regulatory authority. The PNGRB is responsible for authorizing entities for activities such as laying and operating pipelines and city gas distribution networks. Companies in the sector must also comply with a host of health, safety, and labor laws, including the Factories Act, 1948, the Mines Act, 1952, and the Petroleum Rules, 2002. Non-compliance can lead to severe penalties, including license revocation and financial penalties.
