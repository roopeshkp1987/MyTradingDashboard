4.3 Regulatory EnvironmentThe regulatory landscape for the Indian alcoholic beverage industry is primarily governed by the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI), which has introduced significant updates. The Food Safety and Standards (Alcoholic Beverages) First Amendment Regulations, 2025, established new, clear definitions for several categories, including ready-to-drink (RTD) beverages, which are now defined as containing between 0.5% and 15.0% alcohol by volume. Furthermore, the FSSAI has mandated a tolerance limit for declared ethyl alcohol content, setting it at ±0.3% for beverages with up to 20% abv and at ±1.0% for products containing more than 20% abv, ensuring product consistency.
4.3 Regulatory EnvironmentA key regulatory development impacting all manufacturers, including Tilaknagar Industries, is the mandatory labeling of standard drinks on packaging, which becomes enforceable from July 1 of the current year. This rule aims to increase consumer awareness about alcohol consumption. According to the FSSAI, one standard drink in India is defined as containing 10 grams of pure alcohol (ethanol). This translates to approximately 30ml of spirits at 40% ABV or 100ml of wine. For example, a 750ml bottle of a beverage with 8% alcohol content will need to be labeled as containing approximately 4.8 standard drinks. These regulations align with World Health Organization recommendations and signal a move towards greater consumer transparency.
