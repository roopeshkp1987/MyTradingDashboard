Regulatory EnvironmentThe Indian real estate and construction sector is governed by a comprehensive regulatory framework, with the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA), serving as a cornerstone legislation. Key provisions include the mandatory registration of any project on land exceeding 500 square meters or having more than eight units. Furthermore, the act legally requires developers to deposit 70% of funds collected from homebuyers into a separate escrow account to ensure the capital is used for the specified project's construction. The goods and services tax (GST) regime also impacts the sector, with rates rationalized in 2019 to 5% for non-affordable and 1% for affordable housing projects.
