Regulatory EnvironmentThe regulatory landscape for online travel agencies in India is primarily governed by the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) and the Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025. These reg ulations classify OTAs as data fiduciaries and mandate the lawful processing of personal data only after obtaining specific, informed, and unambiguous consent from users. The DPDP Act also imposes strict obligations on data fiduciaries to implement reasonable technical and organizational safeguards to protect personal data. Additiona lly, the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, and guidelines from the Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA) address issues like misleading advertising and unfair trade practices. For GST c ompliance, travel agents are taxed at a standard rate of 18% on their commission or service charges, not the full value of the tour package, as per Rule 32 of the GST rules.
Regulatory EnvironmentWhile there is no mandatory licensing for travel agencies in India, accreditation from the International Air Transport Association (IATA) is necessary for agencies that wish to issue air tickets directly. Many agen cies, however, operate through host agents or consolidators. Approval from the Ministry of Tourism, though optional, can enhance credibility. A key tax regulation impacting OTAs is the Tax Collected at Source (TCS) on overseas tour packages under the Income Tax Act. A TCS of 5% is applicable on the total package cost. GST regis tration is mandatory for travel agencies with a turnover exceeding ₹20 lakh in most states (₹10 lakh in special category states). Furthermore, depending on their service offerings, OTAs may need to comply with state-level tourism regulations, transport and aggregator compliance for ground mobility, and safety norms for experience-based travel.
