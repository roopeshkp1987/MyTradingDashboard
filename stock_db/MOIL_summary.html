OverviewMOIL Limited, a Schedule “A” Miniratna Category-I Public Sector Undertaking, is India's largest producer of manganese ore, headquartered in Nagpur, Maharashtra. The company was originally established in 1896 as the "Central Province Prospecting Syndicate" and was incorporated in its current form on June 22, 1962. MOIL operates 11 mines across Maharashtra and Madhya Pradesh, comprising 8 underground and 3 opencast mines, with its Balaghat mine being the deepest, reaching a depth of about 383 meters. This segment is engaged in the extraction and sale of various grades of manganese ore, including high-grade ore for ferro-manganese production, medium-grade ore for silico-manganese, and blast furnace grade ore for hot metal production.
