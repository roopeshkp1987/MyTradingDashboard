Regulatory EnvironmentThe regulatory framework is a significant driver for the Cocoa Butter Equivalent (CBE) industry, notably shaped by the European Union's Chocolate Directive established in the year 2000. This regulation permits the use of up to 5% non-cocoa vegetable fats in chocolate products, creating a legitimate and substantial addressable market for CBEs across Europe. In In dia, the domestic regulatory environment provides a degree of protection for local manufacturers like Manorama, as Indian regulations specify that CBEs can only be manufactured from 3 types of seeds—Sal, Mango, and Kokum—and impose high import duties on competing Shea-based fats. More rece ntly, the EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) has added further complexity and cost, increasing cocoa expenses by an estimated 4%, which enhances the economic appeal of sustainably-sourced CBEs.
