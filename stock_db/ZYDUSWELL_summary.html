Regulatory EnvironmentThe regulatory landscape for health and wellness products in India is primarily governed by the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI), under the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006. Regulatory EnvironmentAdherence to FSSAI regulations is crucial for all players in the Indian wellness market to ensure consumer trust and avoid penalties. The regulations sti pulate that health supplements can be used to supplement the normal diet of individuals over the age of five years and must contain concentrated sources of nutrients with established physiological benefits. Furthermore, the FS SAI mandates that for products making a nutrient content claim, the individual nutrient level must not be less than fifteen percent of the recommended daily allowance. For product formula tion, companies must ensure that ingredients are listed in the approved schedules of the 2016 Nutraceutical Regulations, and dosages generally should not exceed the Recommended Daily Allowance (RDA) limits set by the Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) unless scientifically justified and approved by the FSSAI. These comprehensive regulations aim to protect consumers and ensure the integrity of health and wellness products in the market.
