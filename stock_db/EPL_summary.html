Regulatory RiskThe English Premier League (EPL) is undergoing a significant regulatory overhaul with the implementation of the Football Governance Act 2025, which establishes an Independent Football Regulator (IFR) and ends the era of self-regulation. T his introduces substantial compliance risks and costs for all 20 clubs. A key change is the transition from the Profitability and Sustainability Rules (PSR), which allowed for losses of up to £105 million over a three-year period, to a stricter Squad Cost Ratio (SCR) mechanism commencing in the 2026/27 season. Un der the new SCR, clubs will be limited to spending 85% of their revenue on squad costs, with a more stringent 70% cap for those competing in UEFA competitions. Penalties for breaching these new financial sustainability regulations are severe, with a "Red Threshold" breach resulting in an automatic six-point deduction, plus an additional one-point penalty for every £6.5 million of overspend, creating significant sporting and financial jeopardy.
Regulatory RiskRegulatory scrutiny is intensifying around the complex ownership structures of many EPL clubs, posing a risk of future sanctions and forced divestment. Research focusing on the 2023-2024 season revealed significant governance vulnerabilities, finding that more than half of the 20 EPL clubs utilize at least one holding company incorporated in an offshore jurisdiction, often in jurisdictions with high secrecy scores. Furthermore, the analysis showed that 7 of the 20 clubs make use of multiple overseas jurisdictions within their ownership chain, complicating efforts to establish ultimate beneficial ownership. Critically, 12 of the 20 clubs had at least 10% of their holdings that could not be formally traced back to their beneficial owners due to secrecy provisions, creating a significant risk for illicit financial flows and reputational damage to the league. The current 'Owners' and Directors' Test' has been deemed insufficient, as it is possible to own up to 25% of an EPL club without undergoing checks on the source of funds.
Market RiskThe league's financial model is facing pricing pressure in its domestic broadcasting market, increasing reliance on more volatile international rights deals.
