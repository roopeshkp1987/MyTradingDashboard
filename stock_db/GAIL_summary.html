Regulatory RiskGAIL faces significant regulatory hurdles from the Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board (PNGRB), which impacts its revenue from the gas transmission business. The PNGRB recently approved an interim tariff of ₹65.69 per mmBtu, a 12% increase from the previous ₹58.60 per mmBtu, but this falls considerably short of the ₹78 per mmBtu sought by the company, creating a revenue gap. GAIL has appealed this order, citing errors in calculation, such as the exclusion of future capital expenditures and the incorrect allocation of gains from pipeline capacity utilization above 75%. The company is entitled to a 12% post-tax return on capital employed through these tariffs, and regulatory decisions that limit this can adversely affect its financial health and investment plans.
